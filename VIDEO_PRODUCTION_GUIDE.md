# Video Production Guide

**FHEVM Example Hub - One-Minute Demonstration Video**

Complete guide for creating the submission video.

## Overview

Two script files provided for video production:

1. **VIDEO_SCRIPT.md** - Full script with timing and visual directions
2. **VIDEO_NARRATION_SCRIPT** - Pure narration without timing

---

## File Descriptions

### VIDEO_SCRIPT.md

**Contents**:
- Complete script with timing codes (0:00-1:00)
- Visual directions for each scene
- Narration text
- Production notes
- Technical requirements

**Best for**: Directors, video editors, production teams

**How to use**:
1. Use timing codes to plan shots
2. Follow visual directions for screen captures
3. Record narration synchronized with timing
4. Add transitions and effects per specifications

### VIDEO_NARRATION_SCRIPT

**Contents**:
- Pure narration text without timing
- Scene-by-scene breakdown
- Full continuous narration
- Speaking tips
- Word count and duration estimates

**Best for**: Voice actors, narrators, text-to-speech conversion

**How to use**:
1. Read directly for narration recording
2. Use for text-to-speech voice generation
3. Share with voice talent
4. Paste into teleprompter systems

## Technical Specifications

### Video Format
- **Resolution**: 1080p (1920x1080) minimum, 4K (3840x2160) recommended
- **Frame Rate**: 30fps or 60fps
- **Codec**: H.264
- **Container**: MP4
- **Bitrate**: 5000-8000 kbps (for streaming quality)

### Audio Format
- **Sample Rate**: 44.1kHz or 48kHz
- **Bitrate**: 128kbps or higher
- **Channels**: Stereo
- **Format**: AAC or MP3

### Duration
- **Target**: Exactly 60 seconds
- **Tolerance**: Â±2 seconds acceptable
- **Speaking Pace**: 55-60 seconds for narration + 0-5 seconds for music

### Color & Styling
- **Primary Colors**: Dark background with bright highlights
- **Font**: Modern, readable (sans-serif recommended)
- **Theme**: Professional, tech-focused
- **Consistency**: Maintain brand colors throughout

## Narration Guidelines

### Speaking Tips
1. **Pace**: Speak naturally at comfortable pace (not too fast, not too slow)
2. **Emphasis**: Stress key numbers: "twelve," "one hundred forty," "thirty thousand," "one hundred," "eighty percent"
3. **Tone**: Professional, confident, enthusiastic
4. **Pausing**: Brief pauses after major sentences for emphasis
5. **Pronunciation**: Clear diction, especially for technical terms

### Technical Terms to Emphasize
- "FHEVM" - Fully Homomorphic Encryption Virtual Machine
- "Encrypted data"
- "Privacy-preserving"
- "Example hub"
- "Automation"
- "Production-ready"

### Recording Quality
- Use quality microphone
- Record in quiet environment
- Avoid background noise
- Ensure consistent volume levels
- Test audio levels before recording
